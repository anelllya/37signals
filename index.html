Why am I a designer?
I find great joy in creating something from nothing. Whenever I face a creative challenge, I dive into exploration, testing possibilities and narrowing the scope to arrive at the strongest solution. I'm relentless when it comes to achieving any goal I set my sights on. I love geeking out over new styles, emerging trends, and fresh takes on old ones. Above all, I love exploring, building, and shipping things.
Why am I working on the web?
I studied Industrial Design, but I started learning HTML and CSS back in 2010, right around the time current web standards were taking shape. Little by little, I found myself falling for the web. Over time, I picked up more CSS and HTML, then SASS, and kept building on what I knew. It felt good. Before long, I was defining websites at work, gaining experience, and loving the results I could create. I've been working on the web ever since. And I created this HTML letter just for you.
Summary of work that I admire
  • Zara - What I love about it is that they introduced an NFC card read step at checkout, it was a long time ago, but it was the first app I ever saw doing that.
  • Carrefour - This was the first app on my phone to introduce a "Zero Paper" feature and honestly, that's the only reason it's still on my list. It felt like a real innovation back then, even though now it's pretty much the norm.
  • The Verge - When it comes to graphic design applied to a website, nothing beats The Verge in my mind. Their use of color, typography, and layout feels bold, thoughtful, and consistently ahead of the curve.
  • Basecamp - I created a free account for one project a long time ago, and I still have it and use it to this day. What I love about it is how naturally the interface aligns with my own way of thinking. I get it instantly, I see everything at a glance, and honestly, that makes me really happy.
