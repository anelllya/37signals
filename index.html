Why am I a designer?
I find great joy in creating something from nothing. Whenever I face a creative challenge, I dive into exploration, testing possibilities and narrowing the scope to arrive at the strongest solution. I'm relentless when it comes to achieving any goal I set my sights on. I love geeking out over new styles, emerging trends, and fresh takes on old ones. Above all, I love exploring, building, and shipping things.
Why am I working on the web?
I studied Industrial Design, but when I started learning HTML and CSS back in 2010, right around the time standards were starting to take shape, I got really into them. Little by little, I picked up more CSS, then SASS, and kept building up on what I knew. It felt good. I started falling for it, and pretty soon I was defining websites at work, gaining experience and loving the results I was able to create. I've been working on the web ever since.

Dear 37signals Team,

I've been waiting nearly a decade for this position to open up. Just the possibility for me to join 37signals feels a bit surreal, not just because of the products you are building, but because of the way you're building them.

The way 37signals approaches product development is exactly how I've always wanted to work. It would be an honor to contribute, learn, and grow with your team.

I believe in async work, in small, powerful teams, and in building software that respects people's time and attention. That's exactly why I'm here.

Ironically - or maybe fatefully - I once worked at a company called System73. Another beautiful prime number. Another team driven by product excellence. It feels like my path is leading me toward 37signals, and I'm ready for it.
